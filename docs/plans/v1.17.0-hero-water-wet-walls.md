# v1.17.0 — Hero water: wet walls & meniscus

> **SHIPPED (2026-06-09, branch `hero-water-14-18`).** New `WetWallSystem` (gpu/wetwall.rs)
> owns a per-wall-texel f32 wetness buffer; a `wetwall_update.wgsl` compute pass runs once
> per frame after the substep loop, reading the *current* `cell_type` classification
> (Liquid cells adjacent to Solid walls) with framerate-corrected decay
> `wetness = max(new_contact, prev * pow(decay, dt*60))`. The `environment.wgsl` WALL branch
> reads wetness → darken/gloss/streak; subtle Fresnel meniscus band at the waterline; contact
> shadow at the floor/wall join. Persists across frames, **clears on Reset** (WetWallSystem
> rebuilt in `recreate_fluid`). All `render.hero.wet_wall.*` Live; conservative defaults.
> Direct foam/spray→wetness coupling **deferred** (the cell-type signal already captures wall
> contacts; `wetness_spray_gain` stubbed at 0). Opus review: **0 must-fix** (1 should-fix on
> paused-state param staleness + nits, all applied). Valid DamBreak on/off A/B (EVAL echo,
> toggle confirmed in console); ~+0.6 ms render. **Visual confidence LOW** — the effect is
> intentionally subtle and the on/off captures were at mismatched camera angles, so the
> still-frame A/B is weak; **this feature most needs live-session sign-off.** Doc migration
> deferred to the final consolidated pass.

> Hero-water series plan 6 of 7. Inherits the *Cross-cutting decisions* in
> [`v1.12.0-hero-water-refraction.md`](v1.12.0-hero-water-refraction.md). **Depends on
> v1.12** — there must be rendered tank walls (in `scene_color`) to wet and a waterline
> to highlight. Benefits from v1.13 foam (foam/spray contact feeds wetness).

## Mission

Make the water feel physically attached to the tank: a persistent **wetness** field on
the walls that darkens/glosses where water has touched and decays over time, a thin
**meniscus** highlight at the liquid–wall waterline, and a soft contact shadow near the
floor/wall join. Done when the slosh capture shows wall impacts leaving visible,
decaying wet streaks and the water reads as contacting the boundary rather than floating.

## Scope

**In:**
- A wetness texture or grid for the tank walls, written each frame from: liquid cells
  neighboring Solid wall cells, particles near a wall, and (if v1.13 is in) spray/foam
  wall contact. Decay: `wetness = max(new_contact, wetness_prev * decay)`.
- Wall rendering reads wetness → darker + glossier (+ optional subtle vertical streak).
- Meniscus: detect wall-adjacent liquid surface cells, render a thin highlight band at
  the waterline with a small Fresnel boost.
- Contact shadow near floor/wall boundaries.
- Debug view: `WetWall` / `MeniscusMask`.

**Out:** physically simulated thin-film drainage; per-droplet rivulets (streaks are a
cheap procedural cue, not simulated flow).

## Approach

Independent accumulation target + wall-shader change; reads cell types and particle
positions. Mostly parallel to v1.15/v1.16.

1. Wetness accumulation target + write pass (contact detection from cell types/particles).
2. Decay over time.
3. Wall material reads wetness (darken/gloss/streak).
4. Meniscus band + contact shadow.
5. Capture Scene 2 (slosh against wall); tune; profiler.

## Watch-outs

- **Wall-adjacency detection** comes from the MAC classification (Solid boundary cells vs
  Liquid neighbors) — that contract exists ([`simulation.md`](../architecture/simulation.md)),
  but confirm the wetness write reads the *current* per-substep classification, which is
  rebuilt every substep (stale cell type is not persistent state).
- **Persistence vs Reset:** the wetness field survives normal frames but must clear when
  Reset rebuilds the tank/fluid (mirror `recreate_fluid`).
- **Subtlety is the brief:** the source plan says "do not overdo" the meniscus. Default
  strengths low; this is a finishing cue, not a feature that should dominate a capture.
- Walls are only minimally textured as of v1.12; if v1.15 raised wall material fidelity,
  coordinate the wetness read with it.
- No readback; wetness is GPU-only.

## Concrete implementation (orchestrator-refined, 2026-06-09)

Grounded in the committed `hero-water-14-18` baseline. Source-of-truth precedence:
runtime Rust/WGSL over this doc. Code anchors below were read, not assumed.

### Where the walls actually render (the wetness read seam)

Two different shaders confusingly share a stem — keep them straight:

- **`shaders/environment.wgsl`** (driven by `gpu/environment.rs`,
  `EnvironmentRenderer`) is the tank floor/walls **mesh prepass**. It draws into
  `scene_color` (linear HDR, location 0) + `scene_depth` (eye distance, location 1)
  in the "hero scene prepass" render pass (`gpu/mod.rs` ~L1131–1173, before the
  water passes). This is the wetness read seam. Its fragment shader (`fs`,
  L40–74) branches on a per-vertex `kind` float: `< 0.5` = FLOOR (checker+grid),
  `< 1.5` = WALL (matte `vec3(0.10,0.12,0.16) * (0.4 + wall_visibility)`),
  else BACKDROP. The mesh is the back wall (z=lo.z), left wall (x=lo.x), and floor
  (y=lo.y); the +x and +z faces are intentionally open (`environment_mesh`,
  environment.rs L190–227). World position is available in the VS (`pos` attribute);
  today only `uv`/`kind`/`eye` are passed to the FS, so the wet-wall read needs
  **world pos passed through to the FS** to index the wetness field.
- **`shaders/env.wgsl`** (`env_sample`) is the shared procedural *sky* function for
  skybox + reflection — NOT the wall material. Do not touch it for wetness.

The darken/gloss/streak edit lands in `environment.wgsl::fs`, in the `kind < 1.5`
WALL branch (and optionally the floor branch for the floor/wall join shadow). The
new bind group entry (wetness storage buffer + its dims/tank-bounds uniform) is
added in `EnvironmentRenderer::new` (environment.rs L70–91 BGL, L84–91 bind group),
and `EnvironmentRenderer` must be rebuilt with the fresh wetness buffer in
`recreate_fluid` (mod.rs L455–463).

### Classification source + the rebuilt-every-substep contract

Cell types live in the GPU storage buffer `cell_type` (host accessor
`GpuFluid::cell_type_buffer()`, fluid.rs L926). It is **rewritten every substep**
by `shaders/classify.wgsl` (`@workgroup_size(64)` `main`), dispatched as section 2
`"d.classify"` (`fluid.dispatch_classify`) inside each substep — see
`record_substep_detailed` mod.rs L862 and the fast-path equivalent. The shader
writes `0=Solid` (any boundary cell: `i/j/k == 0 || == n-1`), `1=Liquid` (occupied
or surface-dilated), `2=Air`. Encoding matches host `CellType`.

Consequence for wetness: the only durable wall-contact signal is **Liquid cells
that 6-neighbour a Solid boundary cell**, read from the *current* `cell_type`
buffer. There is no persistent per-cell contact state in the sim — wetness
persistence is entirely the new target's job. The wetness write pass must run
**after** the per-substep sim loop for the frame has finished (so `cell_type`
reflects the latest substep), i.e. once per frame between `update_diffuse` and the
render prepass, reading the same `cell_type_buffer()` the diffuse system reads.

### Wetness target + write pass (mirror the diffuse system's shape)

Model the new `WetWallSystem` (new file `gpu/wetwall.rs`, registered `mod wetwall;`
in mod.rs L1–11 region) on `gpu/diffuse.rs`:

- **Target:** a `STORAGE | COPY_DST` buffer of `f32` (or packed `u32`), one scalar
  per *wall-surface texel*. Cheapest faithful layout: index by the wall mesh's
  own grid — a 2D array per wall face sized to the grid faces it spans
  (back wall: nx×ny; left wall: nz×ny; floor: nx×nz), concatenated. Dims +
  `tank_lo/tank_hi` go in a small uniform shared with `environment.wgsl` so the
  wall FS maps world pos → wetness index identically to the write pass. Memory is
  tiny (a few × grid-face counts × 4 B ≪ the 12.6 MB diffuse buffer); record it at
  ship time for the gate.
- **Write/decay compute pass** (`shaders/wetwall_update.wgsl`, one invocation per
  wall texel): compute `new_contact` for this texel from (a) the projecting Liquid
  cell adjacent to the Solid wall (read `cell_type`), plus optional gains from
  (b) foam/spray contact (see below). Then
  `wetness[t] = max(new_contact, wetness[t] * wetness_decay)`. `decay` is a
  per-frame multiplier (or framerate-corrected `pow(decay, dt*60)`); default high
  (~0.97) so streaks linger seconds, not frames. Dispatch from a new
  `WetWallSystem::record_step(device, queue, dt)` with its own encoder, called from
  mod.rs right after `update_diffuse` and before the prepass, mirroring
  `DiffuseSystem::record_step` (diffuse.rs L317–358).

### Foam/spray contact source (v1.13 coupling)

`gpu/diffuse.rs` keeps particles in the `particles` storage buffer (pos_type /
vel_age / life vec4 triples, `DIFFUSE_CAPACITY=262_144`) and per-type alive counts
in `counters` (slots 3/4/5 = foam/spray/bubble). Wall impacts are already the spawn
source for spray in `diffuse_emit.wgsl` (L154–183: `impact` = velocity into a SOLID
neighbour, gated by `wall_thresh`). **Recommended for v1.17.0: defer direct
particle→wetness coupling.** The Liquid-neighbours-Solid signal from `cell_type`
already captures every wall contact that spawns spray (same `SOLID`-neighbour test),
so it gives faithful wet streaks without binding the 12.6 MB particle buffer into
the wetwall pass. Wire `wetness_spray_gain` as a registered setting but have it
default to 0 / no-op, and note the follow-up: a later pass can scatter spray
particle positions (read `particles` + a tank-bounds uniform, project onto the
nearest wall texel, `atomicMax`/additive into wetness) to catch airborne spray that
re-wets a wall above the current waterline. Be honest in the migration note that
this coupling is stubbed in v1.17.0, not delivered.

### Reset hook (persistence vs clear)

User-facing Reset (`window.__fluid.reset()` → `lib.rs::reset` L506) calls
`gpu.recreate_fluid` (lib.rs L519), which **rebuilds** the diffuse system from
scratch against fresh buffers (mod.rs L490–501, a fresh zeroed buffer = clean
slate). `GpuContext::reset` (mod.rs L636) only re-seeds particles in place and is
not on the user Reset path. Therefore: **clear the wetness target by rebuilding
`WetWallSystem` (fresh zeroed buffer) inside `recreate_fluid`**, immediately after
the diffuse rebuild (mod.rs ~L500, alongside `self.diffuse = diffuse;
self.diffuse_frame = 0;`). Construct it once in `GpuContext::new` (~L324, beside
the diffuse construction) too. Do NOT clear it in normal frames — that is the
persistence. No clear needed in `GpuContext::reset`.

### Settings (all Live, batch-routed, no per-id plumbing)

`lib.rs` L618 routes **any** id starting with `render.hero.` to
`gpu.set_hero_params(settings.hero_params())`. So register the wet-wall settings
under the `render.hero.wet_wall.*` prefix and they ride that batch route for free —
no new dispatch arm. `set_hero_params` (mod.rs L713) already fans out to composite +
environment + skybox + caustics; add `self.wetwall.set_params(&self.queue, &hero)`
there (the env wall read takes its strengths from `HeroParams`, written into the
shared wet-wall uniform). Registered ids (mirror the caustics block shape,
settings.rs L1192–1339; `Category::Water`, `PanelGroup::Advanced`,
`ApplyClass::Live`):

`render.hero.wet_wall.enabled`, `render.hero.wet_wall.wetness_decay`,
`render.hero.wet_wall.wetness_contact_gain`, `render.hero.wet_wall.wetness_spray_gain`
(default 0, stubbed), `render.hero.wet_wall.darkening_strength`,
`render.hero.wet_wall.gloss_strength`, `render.hero.wet_wall.streak_strength`,
`render.hero.wet_wall.meniscus_enabled`, `render.hero.wet_wall.meniscus_width`,
`render.hero.wet_wall.meniscus_strength`, `render.hero.wet_wall.meniscus_fresnel_boost`,
`render.hero.wet_wall.contact_shadow_enabled`,
`render.hero.wet_wall.contact_shadow_strength`,
`render.hero.wet_wall.contact_shadow_radius`, plus a `render.hero.debug_view` enum
value (or `wet_wall.debug_view`) selecting `WetWall` / `MeniscusMask`.

New `HeroParams` fields (settings.rs struct L178–228, after the caustics block) +
matching reads in `hero_params()` (L1781–1841, mirror the `caustics_*`
`self.f32_or(...)` / `self.u32_or(...) != 0` lines). These fields are read by both
the env wall FS (via the wet-wall uniform) and the wetwall update pass.

### Meniscus + contact shadow

Meniscus is a render-only cue in `environment.wgsl::fs`, not a sim feature: at a
wall texel, detect the waterline as the boundary between wet (high wetness, or the
topmost Liquid-adjacent row) and dry, and add a thin Fresnel-boosted highlight band
of `meniscus_width` centred there, scaled by `meniscus_strength`. Keep defaults low
("do not overdo" is the brief). Contact shadow: in the floor branch (and lower wall
rows), darken within `contact_shadow_radius` of the floor/wall join by
`contact_shadow_strength` (an ambient-occlusion-ish gradient off the seam),
gated by `contact_shadow_enabled`.

### Risks / watch-outs

- The wall FS currently has no world position — must thread `pos` through VS→FS
  (add a `@location` to `VsOut`) for the wetness index; small but touches the
  vertex layout's interpolants.
- World-pos→wetness-texel mapping must match exactly between the write pass and the
  FS read, or streaks will slide off the geometry; share one tank-bounds+dims
  uniform between `WetWallSystem` and `EnvironmentRenderer`.
- `EnvironmentRenderer` is rebuilt in `recreate_fluid`; its new wetness bind group
  must rebind the *fresh* wetwall buffer there or it dangles after Reset.
- Framerate-independent decay: a raw per-frame multiplier makes streaks vanish
  faster at high FPS; prefer `pow(decay, dt*60)` (capture is fixed-step, so verify
  in the live app too).
- Keep the write pass out of the timestamped sim passes (own encoder, like
  diffuse) so it does not perturb `d.classify` timings; record its own
  `gpu.render_ms` delta for the gate.
