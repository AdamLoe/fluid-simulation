# v1.17.0 — Hero water: wet walls & meniscus

> Hero-water series plan 6 of 7. Inherits the *Cross-cutting decisions* in
> [`v1.12.0-hero-water-refraction.md`](v1.12.0-hero-water-refraction.md). **Depends on
> v1.12** — there must be rendered tank walls (in `scene_color`) to wet and a waterline
> to highlight. Benefits from v1.13 foam (foam/spray contact feeds wetness).

## Mission

Make the water feel physically attached to the tank: a persistent **wetness** field on
the walls that darkens/glosses where water has touched and decays over time, a thin
**meniscus** highlight at the liquid–wall waterline, and a soft contact shadow near the
floor/wall join. Done when the slosh capture shows wall impacts leaving visible,
decaying wet streaks and the water reads as contacting the boundary rather than floating.

## Scope

**In:**
- A wetness texture or grid for the tank walls, written each frame from: liquid cells
  neighboring Solid wall cells, particles near a wall, and (if v1.13 is in) spray/foam
  wall contact. Decay: `wetness = max(new_contact, wetness_prev * decay)`.
- Wall rendering reads wetness → darker + glossier (+ optional subtle vertical streak).
- Meniscus: detect wall-adjacent liquid surface cells, render a thin highlight band at
  the waterline with a small Fresnel boost.
- Contact shadow near floor/wall boundaries.
- Debug view: `WetWall` / `MeniscusMask`.

**Out:** physically simulated thin-film drainage; per-droplet rivulets (streaks are a
cheap procedural cue, not simulated flow).

## Approach

Independent accumulation target + wall-shader change; reads cell types and particle
positions. Mostly parallel to v1.15/v1.16.

1. Wetness accumulation target + write pass (contact detection from cell types/particles).
2. Decay over time.
3. Wall material reads wetness (darken/gloss/streak).
4. Meniscus band + contact shadow.
5. Capture Scene 2 (slosh against wall); tune; profiler.

## Watch-outs

- **Wall-adjacency detection** comes from the MAC classification (Solid boundary cells vs
  Liquid neighbors) — that contract exists ([`simulation.md`](../architecture/simulation.md)),
  but confirm the wetness write reads the *current* per-substep classification, which is
  rebuilt every substep (stale cell type is not persistent state).
- **Persistence vs Reset:** the wetness field survives normal frames but must clear when
  Reset rebuilds the tank/fluid (mirror `recreate_fluid`).
- **Subtlety is the brief:** the source plan says "do not overdo" the meniscus. Default
  strengths low; this is a finishing cue, not a feature that should dominate a capture.
- Walls are only minimally textured as of v1.12; if v1.15 raised wall material fidelity,
  coordinate the wetness read with it.
- No readback; wetness is GPU-only.

## Settings (Water tab → Wet Wall group, all Live)

Mirror `[render.hero_water.wet_wall]`: `wet_wall.enabled`, `wetness_decay`,
`wetness_contact_gain`, `wetness_spray_gain`, `darkening_strength`, `gloss_strength`,
`streak_strength`, `meniscus_enabled`, `meniscus_width`, `meniscus_strength`,
`meniscus_fresnel_boost`, `contact_shadow_enabled`, `contact_shadow_strength`,
`contact_shadow_radius`.

## Exit gate

- WSL build + `cargo test --lib` green.
- Scene 2 (slosh) capture: wall impacts leave decaying wetness; meniscus reads at the
  waterline; water looks attached to the boundary.
- Wetness decay + meniscus sliders change the look live.
- `gpu.render_ms` delta + wetness-target memory recorded.
