# v1.13.0 — Hero water: persistent foam / diffuse particles

> Hero-water series plan 2 of 7. Inherits the *Cross-cutting decisions* in
> [`v1.12.0-hero-water-refraction.md`](v1.12.0-hero-water-refraction.md). This plan is
> the **most independent** of the series — it's a separate GPU particle system that
> renders over whatever water surface exists (current composite or, later, the v1.14
> mesh). It can be built before or after v1.14.

## Mission

Replace the current speed-weighted "whitewater" tint with a **persistent diffuse-water
particle system** — foam (surface flecks), spray (airborne droplets), and bubbles
(submerged air) that are *born* at impacts/breaking surfaces, advect, and *decay* over
time instead of flashing only while water is fast. Done when the corner-churn and
slosh captures show whitewater that persists for a second or more after the impact and
fades naturally, and emission/lifetime are tunable live.

This directly targets the brief's diagnosis
([`fluid-system-llm-brief.md`](../architecture/fluid-system-llm-brief.md) §"Why current
attempts fall short"): "a fast-water mask alone tends to look like white tint rather
than foam."

## Scope

**In:**
- A `DiffuseParticle` GPU storage buffer (`pos_type`, `vel_age`, `params` — one shared
  buffer with a `type` field), persistent across frames.
- GPU alive/emit **counters** via integer atomics (consistent with the fixed-point
  `scatter.wgsl` pattern; no float atomics, no readback).
- **Emission compute** driven by existing sim buffers (cell types, MAC face velocities,
  pressure, divergence): surface speed at liquid/air interface, wall-impact (velocity
  into a Solid neighbor), and a turbulence/vorticity term. Stochastic spawn via
  `hash(seed, particle_idx, frame_index)`.
- **Update compute**: age/lifetime kill, advect with sampled grid velocity, bubble
  buoyancy, spray ballistic gravity + drag, type transitions (spray re-entering liquid →
  foam/bubble).
- **Render pass** over the water composite: soft additive foam patches, reflective spray
  dots, milky bubbles. Depth-tested against the shared depth.
- Debug views (`FoamParticles`, `SprayParticles`, `BubbleParticles`) + stats counts
  (alive per type) surfaced through the throttled timing path.

**Out:** mist (add later — start with foam/spray/bubble per the source plan's "minimum
acceptable"); any coupling back into the solver (diffuse particles do **not** conserve
mass or affect pressure — render-only state).

## Approach

Mostly parallel to v1.12/v1.14 since it owns new files
(`gpu/diffuse.rs`, `shaders/diffuse_emit.wgsl`, `diffuse_update.wgsl`,
`diffuse_render.wgsl`) and only *reads* sim buffers.

1. Buffer + counters + a frame-index uniform (needed for the spawn hash — see watch-outs).
2. Emission compute with the simplified first-pass signals
   (`is_surface = Liquid cell with Air neighbor`, `speed = |grid_velocity|`,
   `impact = velocity into Solid`). Gate behind `diffuse.emit_enabled`.
3. Update compute (age/advect/buoyancy/drag/transitions).
4. Render pass + the existing whitewater tint retired or demoted to a fallback.
5. Capture Scene 4 (corner churn) + Scene 2 (slosh); tune emission/lifetime; profiler.

## Watch-outs

- **Which signals actually exist?** Cell types, face velocities, pressure, and divergence
  are all live buffers ([`simulation.md`](../architecture/simulation.md)). **Vorticity is
  not** — it needs a derived term or a cheap curl-of-face-velocity estimate. **Compression
  = negative pre-projection divergence**, but the divergence buffer may be consumed/overwritten
  by the pressure solve; confirm whether a *pre-projection* divergence is readable or
  approximate it. Don't assume the ChatGPT emission formula's inputs are all present.
- **A frame index uniform is required** for deterministic stochastic spawning. The app
  runs a fixed-dt accumulator but doesn't currently expose a frame counter to shaders;
  add one. (`Math.random`/wall-clock are not available to the determinism path.)
- **Persistence vs Reset.** The diffuse buffer survives normal frames but must be cleared
  when Reset-class settings rebuild the fluid (mirror the renderer-recreation path in
  `recreate_fluid`).
- **Budget.** `emit_budget_per_frame` + `max_particles` cap GPU cost; `log`/stats must
  report when emission is clamped so "looks calm" isn't actually "hit the cap."
- Counts to the stats overlay go through the **throttled** timing readback, never a
  per-frame map.

## Settings (Water tab → Foam/Diffuse group, all Live)

Mirror the ChatGPT `[render.hero_water.diffuse]` block, flattened to dotted ids:
`diffuse.enabled`, `diffuse.max_particles`, `diffuse.emit_rate`, `emit_budget_per_frame`,
`surface_speed_threshold/gain`, `wall_impact_threshold/gain`, `vorticity_threshold/gain`,
`compression_threshold/gain`, and per-type
`{foam,bubble,spray}_{lifetime_min,lifetime_max,radius,alpha,...}`,
`foam_decay/advection/surface_lock`, `bubble_buoyancy/advection/milkiness`,
`spray_drag/reflection_strength`, `random_seed`. (~40 settings; all Live.)

## Exit gate

- WSL build + `cargo test --lib` green.
- Scene 4 (corner churn): whitewater persists ≳1s after impact and decays — captured
  before/after vs the speed-mask composite.
- Emission and lifetime sliders change the look live.
- `gpu.render_ms` + new diffuse-pass cost recorded; emission-cap clamping logged.
- No readback added to the normal frame.

**Engineering deviations / decisions (within granted judgment):**

- **Fixed-capacity ring buffer, not realloc.** `render.diffuse.max_particles` is a
  Live *active cap* inside a compile-time `DIFFUSE_CAPACITY` (262144, ~12.6 MB), and
  the emitter recycles slots via a persistent integer-atomic ring cursor. Keeps the
  whole settings block Live (no Reset) per the cross-cutting decision.
- **Emission signals are the present ones only.** Surface speed (cell-centered MAC
  speed at a liquid/air interface) and wall-impact (velocity into a Solid neighbor);
  a cheap fast-interior term seeds bubbles. Vorticity and pre-projection divergence
  were *not* added as sim buffers (the watch-out's flagged risk) — they weren't
  needed for a convincing result and would have cost a sim-side buffer + pass.
- **Run cadence.** emit+update run once per rendered frame (own encoder, summed
  substep dt), not per substep — render-only state doesn't need substep fidelity.
- **Essential settings set (~17), full stats overlay.** Per the scoping decision:
  the functional knobs (enable, cap, rate, budget, the two threshold/gain signal
  pairs, per-type lifetimes, shared radius/alpha, buoyancy, drag, debug view, seed),
  and counts threaded all the way into `stats_json.gpu.diffuse` + the profiler line.
- **Depth.** Particles depth-test against the shared scene depth (environment +
  wireframe), depth-write off; they render *over* the composite (not occluded by the
  water surface itself, whose front isn't in the depth buffer). Good for foam/spray;
  submerged bubbles read slightly milky-over rather than truly occluded.

**Exit gate results:**

- WSL `cargo build --target wasm32-unknown-unknown` and `cargo test --lib` (27 tests) green.
- `tools/capture.mjs` on the default churning scene shows persistent white foam on
  the agitated surface plus spray flecks thrown into the air that linger and settle
  (persistence), with the blue water composite visible beneath — not a speed-mask
  whiteout. (First tuning pass over-emitted ~40k particles and blanketed the tank;
  conservative emission defaults brought it to a tasteful ~3k.)
- `render.diffuse.*` are Live (prefix-matched in `set_setting` → rebuild
  `DiffuseParams` → `set_diffuse_params`; returns `true`, no reset), wired identically
  to the v1.12 hero settings.
- `gpu.render_ms` ≈ 0.34 ms with the diffuse pass active (the diffuse render carries
  the frame's `render_end` timestamp); alive-per-type + emitted/clamped surface in
  `stats_json.gpu.diffuse` and the throttled profiler line, with a ⚠ budget-clamp
  callout when emission hits the per-frame cap. Default budget left headroom (no clamp).
- No readback added to the normal frame — counts flow only through the throttled
  `timing.rs` path.
