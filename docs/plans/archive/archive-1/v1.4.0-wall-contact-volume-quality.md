# v1.4.0 - wall contact and volume quality

## Mission

Fix the simulation feel issues that make water cling to walls or ceilings after a dam
break, and clarify the compactness/volume model before the settings UI tries to
explain it. The target behavior is simple: with `physics.wall_friction = 0`, fluid
slides freely along any tank wall and does not remain pasted to the ceiling. Done means
the cling has a reproduced case, a diagnosis, a principled fix, and captures/tests that
show the new behavior.

## Scope

In scope:

- Use dam break as the primary repro.
- Distinguish visual particle overlap from real particle velocity/cell retention.
- Audit G2P recovery, clamp-inside behavior, zero-normal velocity, tangential damping,
  boundary face zeroing, pressure boundary convention, classification, surface dilation,
  volume correction, CFL, and tank rotation effects.
- Preserve the closed-tank no-normal-flow boundary model unless an explicit replacement
  is planned.
- Make `wall_friction = 0` mean free tangential slide. Higher friction can still drag.
- Investigate settled volume/compactness enough to define the settings model for
  v1.5.0.

Out of scope:

- Bounce/restitution controls.
- Negative-pressure tricks.
- Special floor/ceiling/wall behavior based on current gravity direction.
- Source/drain or open boundaries.
- Broad settings UI restructuring; v1.5.0 owns that.

## Approach

### Wave A - reproduce and instrument

- Capture a dam-break run with `wall_friction = 0` that shows the ceiling/wall cling:
  one-particle-thick layers on the ceiling, slow downward peeling, or long wall
  residence after the water should slide down.
- Capture or log enough state to classify the symptom:
  particle positions/velocity near the wall, liquid cell classification near the wall,
  wall contact recovery, divergence/pressure behavior, and dropped sim time.
- Compare against `wall_friction = 1` so the setting's visible effect is not guessed.

### Wave B - isolate mechanisms

Check each mechanism independently:

- **Particle recovery** - escaped particles clamp inside and normal velocity is zeroed.
  Confirm tangential velocity is preserved when friction is zero.
- **Explicit wall friction** - near-wall tangential damping only applies above zero.
- **Grid boundaries** - solid-adjacent/domain-edge face velocities are zeroed without
  creating tangential stickiness.
- **Pressure convention** - solid Neumann/no-normal-flow stays intact and does not trap
  ceiling layers through classification artifacts.
- **Classification/dilation/volume correction** - near-wall cells do not keep liquid
  classified in places where particles should fall away.
- **CFL/timestep** - velocity clamping is not creating artificial slow peeling.
- **Tank rotation** - gravity/tank transforms are not making "ceiling" ambiguous in the
  dam-break repro.

### Wave C - implement the smallest principled fix

The expected product result is free slip, not bounce. Candidate fix areas include:

- Adjust recovery so normal correction does not erase downhill/tangential motion.
- Narrow or change near-wall damping so friction zero is genuinely no tangential drag.
- Change classification/dilation near solid boundaries if one-cell liquid skins are
  being preserved after particles have detached.
- Tune volume correction so it fights compaction without pinning thin wall layers.

The implementer should only touch the mechanism the investigation identifies. Do not
"make it look better" by broadly weakening closed-tank boundaries.

### Wave D - compactness brief for v1.5.0

Record what each compactness-related knob actually affects after the fix:

- `render.particle_size` - visual only.
- `particles.count` and seeding - initial mass/distribution.
- `physics.rest_density`, `physics.volume_stiffness`, `physics.drift_clamp` - volume
  correction / anti-clump behavior.
- `classify.liquid_threshold`, `classify.surface_dilation` - liquid-cell inclusion and
  continuity.
- `solver.pressure_iterations` - incompressibility quality and performance.

This brief can be in the agent report or a temporary plan note, but the durable result
must migrate to architecture/decisions when shipped.

## Exit gate

- Repro capture exists for the old dam-break cling behavior.
- Diagnosis names the active mechanism when `wall_friction = 0`.
- A fix ships or the exact blocker is documented.
- New behavior capture shows water sliding/falling off walls and ceiling in the dam
  break, with no bounce/restitution requirement.
- `wall_friction = 1` still visibly differs from `wall_friction = 0`.
- `cd /home/adamg/fluid-simulation/app && cargo build --target wasm32-unknown-unknown`
  passes.
- `cd /home/adamg/fluid-simulation/app && cargo test --lib` passes, with host tests
  added where practical for classification/boundary semantics.

## Discipline rules

- Do not equate "wall friction zero" with "all wall effects disabled." Closed tank
  walls still block normal flow.
- Do not special-case floor/ceiling by gravity direction.
- Do not tune the symptom away without evidence of the mechanism.
- Preserve integer/fixed-point P2G and the CG pressure-solver invariants.

## Migration notes (filled in at ship time)

Shipped 2026-06-07.

### Ship record

- Diagnosis confirmed: the active `wall_friction=0` cling mechanism was implicit G2P
  drag from boundary-zeroed MAC faces. Boundary enforcement zeroes domain-edge /
  Solid-adjacent faces; the old G2P gather interpolated those zeros into particles near
  walls and ceilings. Host tests reproduce the damped samples: wall-adjacent tangential
  velocity retained about 55%, and ceiling-away normal velocity retained about 5%.
- Fix shipped: `g2p.wgsl` now excludes static wall/domain-edge face stencils from
  `sample_u/sample_v/sample_w` and renormalizes the remaining weights. Final and saved
  velocities use the identical wall-aware gather, so FLIP deltas stay consistent.
  Boundary passes and recovery still enforce closed-tank no-normal-flow.
- Pressure semantics unchanged: no pressure clamp, no negative-pressure trick, no
  active-set change, no bounce/restitution, no floor/ceiling special case.
- Host tests added in `app/crates/fluid-lab/src/sim/mod.rs`:
  `wall_aware_mac_gather_preserves_tangential_velocity_near_static_wall` and
  `wall_aware_mac_gather_preserves_away_from_ceiling_normal_velocity`.
- Gates:
  - `cd /home/adamg/fluid-simulation/app && cargo build --target wasm32-unknown-unknown`
    passed.
  - `cd /home/adamg/fluid-simulation/app && cargo test --lib` passed: 18 passed,
    0 failed.
  - `cd /home/adamg/fluid-simulation/app && ./run.sh` rebuilt dev WASM for browser
    capture and served `http://localhost:5184/`.
  - `git diff --check` passed.
- Real-GPU capture evidence:
  - `captures/v1.4-fixed-dambreak-wall-friction-0.png` plus
    `captures/v1.4-fixed-dambreak-wall-friction-0.png.frames/` and console log.
    Configured by harness EVAL to `scene.preset=1`, `physics.wall_friction=0`; final
    stats show `gpu-timestamp`, 64x64x64, 252875 particles, 31792 liquid cells. Frames
    show the dam-break splash falling/sliding into a low pool without a persistent
    ceiling or wall sheet.
  - `captures/v1.4-fixed-dambreak-wall-friction-1.png` plus
    `captures/v1.4-fixed-dambreak-wall-friction-1.png.frames/` and console log.
    Same scene/timing with `physics.wall_friction=1`; final stats show 45139 liquid
    cells and frames retain a visibly taller rough wall-adjacent mass/sheet. The
    setting still visibly differs.
  - `captures/v1.4-fixed-wall-friction-0.png*` was an earlier valid GPU boot/run
    capture but did not receive the dam-break EVAL due Windows `cmd.exe` quoting; use
    the `fixed-dambreak-*` captures as the acceptance evidence.
- Compactness brief for v1.5:
  - `render.particle_size` is visual only.
  - `particles.count` and scene seeding set initial mass/distribution and require
    Reset.
  - `physics.rest_density`, `physics.volume_stiffness`, and `physics.drift_clamp`
    control volume correction / anti-clump divergence bias.
  - `classify.liquid_threshold` and `classify.surface_dilation` control liquid-cell
    inclusion; default dilation remains 0.
  - `solver.pressure_iterations` controls incompressibility quality and performance,
    not visual particle overlap.
- Durable doc migration:
  - `architecture/simulation.md` records wall-aware G2P sampling, recovery semantics,
    and the compactness model.
  - `architecture/settings.md` records `physics.wall_friction` and compactness knob
    semantics.
  - `decisions/simulation.md` records the free-slip wall-aware G2P rationale.
  - `architecture/pressure-solver.md` and `decisions/pressure.md` were not touched
    because pressure boundary semantics did not change.
